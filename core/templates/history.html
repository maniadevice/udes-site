History

Violent Conflicts

In the year 2013, ethnic violence erupted in the Central African Republic after months of political unrest. By the end of 2014, the country was de facto partitioned with the anti-Balaka controlling the south and west, from which most Muslims had evacuated, and ex-Seleka groups controlling the north and east. Religious cleansing and human rights abuses forced thousands of Muslims to flee the country.

In May 2014, it was reported that around 600,000 people in CAR were internally displaced with 160,000 of these in the capital Bangui. The Muslim population of Bangui dropped 99% from 138,000 to 900. By May 2014, 100,000 people had fled to neighbouring Cameroon, the Democratic Republic of Congo and Chad. As of 2017, there are more than 1.1 million displaced people in a country of about 5 million people, the highest ever recorded in the country, with about half a million refugees outside CAR and about 600,000 internally displaced. Cameroon hosted the most refugees, more than 135,000.

Refugee Camps

The UN has at least three refugee camps setup near the Cameroon-CAR borders. The largest of which holds nearly 100,000 people. Mr. Hamid Aboubakar, the president of UDES, first visited this camp in the year 2014. He returned to the UK and wrote to the mosques in Leicester, asking for help and was greeted with a tremendous response. He was also kindly supported by the charity, Caravan of Mercy based in Leicester, UK. Together, we managed to channel the donations into crucial support, the refugees were in need of.


Every year we provide basic medication, meat, rice, oil and other necessary ingredients during our annual visits in the month of Ramadan. We have also provided clothing for children and set up a Mosque and a Madrasah for both their general and religious education. We employed three teachers for the education of the refugee children. Origin of UDES

In 2018, Hamid Aboubakar decided to register the charity UDES in Cameroon, to be able to further his efforts. He has received support from his friend Mr. Bobbo Mahmud, who is acting as a vice-president of the charity. Bobbo Mahmud already works on projects to support orphan children. He lives in Cameroon and is able to assess the ground situation in the refugee camp and provide support.

UDES sincerely appeals to you for help, so we can help them. A charitable organisation can only succeed through a collective effort. We have a duty towards these people and this charity was born out of this fact. We are striving to make little differences in their lives and instill positive beliefs about their future. If Allah wills, we plan to introduce a few more sustainable projects so they can live their lives with dignity. We urge you to help these efforts bear fruits with your generous donations.
